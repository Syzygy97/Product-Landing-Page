VIEWS

Pillars of Creation

These towering tendrils of cosmic dust and gas sit at the heart of M16, or the Eagle Nebula. The aptly named Pillars of Creation, are part of an active star-forming region within the nebula and hide newborn stars in their wispy columns.

Carina Nebula

A large, complex area of bright and dark nebulosity in the constellation Carina, located in the Carina–Sagittarius Arm of the Milky Way galaxy.

Halley's Comet

Discovered by Edmond Halley in 1705, Halley's comet is one of the most famous comets in history. Also called comet 1p/Halley, it is the first comet whose return was predicted and, almost three centuries later, the first to be imaged up close by interplanetary spacecraft.

Lunar Eclipse

When Earth is positioned precisely between the Moon and Sun, Earth’s shadow falls upon the surface of the Moon, dimming it and sometimes turning the lunar surface a striking red over the course of a few hours.

Andromeda Galaxy

Andromeda Galaxy is the closest large spiral galaxy to ours. Excluding the Large and Small Magellanic Clouds, visible from Earth’s Southern Hemisphere, the Andromeda galaxy is the brightest external galaxy you can see. At 2.5 million light-years, it’s the most distant thing most of us humans can see with the unaided eye.

Planet Saturn

With its gold color and stunning rings, Saturn is quite a planetary gem. Saturn is the 2nd largest of the 8 planets and is about 10 times as wide as Earth. Despite it size, it is actually the lightest planet in the solar system. Having a confirmed 53 moons, there is a high possibility that these moons can be seen while observing Saturn!

Solar Warning
  • Never look directly at the Sun with the naked eye or with an optic (unless you have the proper solar filter). Permanent and irreversible eye damage may result.
  • Never use your optic to project an image of the Sun onto any surface. Internal heat build-up can damage the optic and any accessories attached to it.
  • Never leave your optic unsupervised. Make sure an adult who is familiar with the correct operating procedures is with your optic at all times, especially when children are present.
